Understanding the Treatment

Orthopaedic PRF Therapy

Platelet-Rich Fibrin (PRF) represents a breakthrough in orthopaedic regenerative medicine. Unlike traditional PRP, PRF produces a natural fibrin scaffold that concentrates your body's most powerful growth factors and releases them gradually over an extended period — promoting genuine tissue repair at the cellular level.

This sustained-release mechanism is what sets PRF apart. Rather than a single burst of healing signals, PRF provides continuous biological stimulation that mirrors your body's natural repair process. Unlike cortisone injections, which merely mask pain while allowing further degeneration, PRF addresses the root cause of musculoskeletal conditions by actively regenerating damaged tissue.

Our orthopaedic PRF protocols are guided by diagnostic imaging and delivered with precision to the exact site of injury, ensuring optimal growth factor concentration where it matters most.

Key Advantage

PRF provides up to 14 days of continuous growth factor release, compared to just hours with traditional PRP treatments — enabling deeper, more sustained tissue regeneration.

The Science

How PRF Promotes Healing

Growth Factors

PRF concentrates essential growth factors including PDGF (platelet-derived growth factor), TGF-B (transforming growth factor beta), and VEGF (vascular endothelial growth factor) — each playing a critical role in tissue repair, collagen synthesis, and new blood vessel formation.

PDGF TGF-B VEGF

Fibrin Matrix

The fibrin scaffold acts as a natural three-dimensional matrix that traps platelets, leukocytes, and growth factors at the injury site. This structure provides a sustained, slow-release delivery system that supports healing over days rather than hours.

Natural Scaffold Slow Release

Stem Cell Activation

PRF growth factors recruit and activate mesenchymal stem cells already present in your tissues. These multipotent cells differentiate into the specific cell types needed — cartilage, tendon, or bone — to repair the damaged area naturally.

Cell Recruitment Differentiation

Your Treatment Journey

Treatment Process

Initial Assessment

Comprehensive musculoskeletal examination, medical history review, and discussion of your pain patterns, functional limitations, and treatment goals.

Diagnostic Imaging

Ultrasound or MRI imaging to precisely identify the site and extent of tissue damage, ensuring targeted treatment delivery.

Blood Collection

A small volume of blood is drawn from your arm using a standard venepuncture technique — similar to a routine blood test.

PRF Preparation

Your blood is processed using a specialised low-speed centrifugation protocol to produce a concentrated fibrin matrix rich in platelets and growth factors.

Guided Injection

The PRF concentrate is injected precisely into the affected joint, tendon, or soft tissue under ultrasound guidance for maximum accuracy.

Recovery Protocol

Personalised rehabilitation plan including activity modification, progressive loading, and follow-up appointments to monitor your healing progress.

Treatment Comparison

PRF vs Cortisone Injections

PRF Therapy

  • Promotes genuine tissue repair and regeneration
  • Uses your own blood — no synthetic chemicals
  • Long-lasting results that improve over months
  • No risk of cartilage degradation or tendon weakening
  • Can be repeated safely without diminishing returns
  • Addresses the underlying cause, not just symptoms

Cortisone Injections

  • Masks pain without addressing the underlying cause
  • Synthetic steroid with potential systemic side effects
  • Temporary relief lasting weeks, then pain returns
  • Can accelerate cartilage breakdown with repeated use
  • Limited to 3-4 injections per joint per year
  • May weaken tendons and increase rupture risk

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Most patients experience only mild discomfort during the injection, comparable to a standard joint injection. We use local anaesthesia at the injection site for comfort. Some patients report mild soreness and swelling for 2-5 days after the procedure, which is a normal part of the healing response and typically resolves on its own.

The number of sessions depends on the severity and nature of your condition. Most patients benefit from a series of 2-3 treatments spaced 4-6 weeks apart. Some conditions may respond well to a single treatment, while more chronic or advanced cases may require additional sessions. Your personalised treatment plan will be determined during your initial assessment.

In many cases, PRF therapy can help patients avoid or delay surgical intervention. It is particularly effective for early-to-moderate osteoarthritis, tendinopathy, and partial tears. However, severe structural damage such as complete ligament ruptures or advanced bone-on-bone arthritis may still require surgical management. We always provide an honest assessment of whether PRF is the right option for your specific condition.

While both use your own blood, PRF (Platelet-Rich Fibrin) is a next-generation advancement over PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma). PRF is produced without anticoagulants, resulting in a natural fibrin matrix that releases growth factors slowly over 10-14 days. PRP delivers growth factors in a single burst that dissipates within hours. The fibrin scaffold in PRF also provides a structural framework that supports tissue regeneration more effectively.

Most patients can return to light daily activities within 24-48 hours. We recommend avoiding strenuous exercise and heavy loading of the treated area for 1-2 weeks. Gentle movement and walking are encouraged from day one. A structured rehabilitation programme will guide your return to full activity, typically between 4-8 weeks depending on the condition treated. Your practitioner will provide specific guidance based on your treatment site.
