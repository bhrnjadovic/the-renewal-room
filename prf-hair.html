The Science

Understanding Hair Loss

Hair loss is one of the most common concerns affecting millions of people worldwide. Whether caused by genetics, hormonal changes, stress, nutritional deficiencies, or ageing, thinning hair can significantly impact confidence and quality of life.

Traditional treatments often rely on medications with ongoing side effects or invasive surgical procedures. Platelet-Rich Fibrin (PRF) therapy offers a powerful, natural alternative — harnessing your body's own regenerative biology to stimulate follicle activity and promote genuine hair regrowth without synthetic chemicals or surgery.

50% of men and 40% of women

experience noticeable hair loss by age 50, making it one of the most widespread aesthetic concerns globally.

The Mechanism

How PRF Stimulates Hair Growth

PRF (Platelet-Rich Fibrin) is prepared from a small sample of your own blood, processed to concentrate platelets, white blood cells, and growth factors within a natural fibrin matrix. When injected into the scalp at areas of thinning, this fibrin scaffold provides a sustained, slow release of growth factors directly to the hair follicles.

Unlike PRP, which releases growth factors rapidly and dissipates, PRF's fibrin architecture acts as a biological reservoir — delivering regenerative signals over days to weeks. This prolonged stimulation reactivates dormant follicles, extends the active growth phase (anagen), increases blood supply to the scalp, and promotes the formation of thicker, stronger hair shafts.

The result is a genuinely restorative process that works with your biology, not against it.

Who Is It For

Ideal Candidates

Good Candidates

  • Early to moderate stage hair thinning (hair follicles are still present but miniaturised)
  • Male or female pattern hair loss (androgenetic alopecia)
  • Stress-related or post-partum hair shedding (telogen effluvium)
  • Patients seeking a natural, drug-free approach to hair restoration
  • Those looking to complement hair transplant surgery for enhanced results

Not Recommended For

  • Complete baldness with no remaining follicles (PRF cannot regenerate absent follicles)
  • Active scalp infections, open wounds, or untreated skin conditions
  • Blood disorders, platelet dysfunction, or anticoagulant therapy
  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding
  • Hair loss caused by autoimmune conditions (requires specialist assessment first)

Your Journey

Treatment Process

Scalp Assessment

Comprehensive evaluation of your scalp health, hair density, and thinning pattern. We discuss your medical history, goals, and determine whether PRF is the right approach for you.

Blood Collection

A small sample of your blood is drawn — similar to a routine blood test. This typically takes just a few minutes and requires no special preparation.

PRF Processing

Your blood is placed in a specialised centrifuge that separates and concentrates the platelet-rich fibrin layer. No additives or anticoagulants are used — the result is 100% natural.

Scalp Injection

The concentrated PRF is carefully injected into targeted areas of thinning using fine needles. Topical anaesthesia is applied beforehand to ensure comfort throughout the procedure.

Post-Treatment Care

You receive detailed aftercare instructions. There is no downtime — most patients resume daily activities immediately. Mild redness or tenderness at the injection sites typically resolves within 24–48 hours.

Both PRF and PRP are derived from your own blood, but they differ in preparation and efficacy. PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) uses anticoagulants during processing and delivers a rapid burst of growth factors that dissipates quickly. This sustained delivery provides longer-lasting stimulation of hair follicles, making PRF the more advanced and effective option for hair regeneration.
